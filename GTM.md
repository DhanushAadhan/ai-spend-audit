# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target Customer

**Primary:** Early-stage startups (5–50 people) using 3+ AI tools simultaneously
**Secondary:** Freelance developers and indie hackers managing multiple subscriptions
**Tertiary:** CTOs and engineering managers at mid-size companies ($500k+ ARR)

### Customer Pain Point
Most startup teams don't know their exact AI spend. Bills come from 4–6 different vendors,
paid on different cards, by different people. Nobody audits it until burn rate becomes a crisis.

---

## Distribution Channels (Ranked by Priority)

### Channel 1 — Product Hunt Launch
- Launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday (highest traffic days)
- Title: "AuditAI — Find out how much your team wastes on AI tools"
- Target: #1 Product of the Day
- Expected: 500–2000 visitors on launch day

### Channel 2 — Hacker News Show HN
- Post: "Show HN: I built a tool that audits your AI tool spend"
- Include real savings example in the post (e.g. "Found $4,800/year in savings for a 10-person team")
- Expected: 300–1000 visitors if it hits front page

### Channel 3 — Twitter/X Dev Community
- Tweet thread showing a real audit walkthrough with screenshots
- Tag relevant accounts: @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @adamwathan
- Use hashtags: #buildinpublic #indiehacker #startups

### Channel 4 — Reddit
- r/startups, r/SaaS, r/webdev, r/ChatGPT
- Post a genuine "I audited our AI spend and found this" case study
- Link to AuditAI as the tool used

### Channel 5 — Cold Outreach
- LinkedIn DMs to CTOs/founders of 20–100 person startups
- Message: "I built a free AI spend auditor — took 2 mins, found $X in savings. Want to try?"

## Pricing Strategy (Post-MVP)

**Free tier:** Full audit, shareable URL, 3 audits/month
**Pro ($9/month):** Unlimited audits, team comparison, historical tracking, CSV export
**Team ($29/month):** Multiple team members, Slack alerts when pricing changes, API access

### Why Freemium
- Audit results are inherently shareable (CTOs forward to their team)
- Viral loop: each shared audit URL brings new users
- Low friction for first audit = high conversion to email capture

## Metrics to Track

- Audits completed per day
- Email capture rate (audits → leads)
- Shareable URL click-through rate
- Average savings identified per audit
- D7 retention (do users come back to re-audit?)
