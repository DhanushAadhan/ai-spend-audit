# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

## Overview

Spoke with 3 people who manage or pay for AI tools in their work.
Interviews conducted: 07–09 May 2026 (in person / WhatsApp / LinkedIn DM)

---

## Interview Script Used

1. "How many AI tools does your team currently pay for?"
2. "Do you know your exact monthly spend on all of them combined?"
3. "Has anyone on your team ever audited or reviewed which tools are actually being used?"
4. "What would make you switch from one AI tool to another?"
5. "If a tool showed you exactly how much you could save — would you trust it? What would make you trust it?"
6. "Would you share the results with your team or manager?"

---

## Interview 1

**Name:** Karthi
**Role:** Freelance Developer
**Date:** 08 May 2026
**Method:** WhatsApp voice note

**Key answers:**
- Uses: Cursor Pro + ChatGPT Plus = ~$40/month personally
- Did not know exact spend until asked — had to check bank statement
- Never audited which features he actually uses
- Would switch if savings > $15/month "it's not worth the hassle for less"
- Would trust a tool more if it showed the source URLs for pricing data
- Would definitely share results — "I'd send it to my manager as ammunition"

**Key insight:** People underestimate their spend. The act of seeing the number is itself valuable.

## Interview 2

**Name:** Mani
**Role:**  startup
**Date:** 08 May 2026
**Method:** 15-min LinkedIn call

**Key answers:**
- Team uses: Cursor Business (8 seats) + GitHub Copilot Business (12 seats) + ChatGPT Team (12 seats)
- Estimated spend: "around $800/month" — actual: $1,136/month
- Last reviewed AI spend: "never formally, just when someone asks"
- Biggest frustration: duplicate tools — "half the team uses Cursor, half uses Copilot, nobody knows which is better"
- Would switch if tool gave a clear side-by-side comparison, not just "switch to X"
- Would share publicly on Twitter if savings were significant ("good content")

**Key insight:** Teams are paying for overlapping tools without realizing it. Deduplication is a huge opportunity.

## Interview 3

**Name:** Gopal
**Role:** Solo Founder
**Date:** 09 May 2026
**Method:** In person

**Key answers:**
- Uses: Claude Pro + ChatGPT Plus + Gemini Advanced = $60/month
- Uses all three "for different things" but couldn't clearly explain the difference
- Considered cancelling one but "didn't want to lose access"
- Would trust savings recommendations if they came with a 30-day trial suggestion
- Biggest ask: "Tell me which one to keep if I could only keep one"

**Key insight:** Solo users feel locked in by fear of missing out. A "which one should I keep" recommendation is more valuable than a savings number.

---

## Patterns Across All 3 Interviews

1. **Nobody knows their exact spend** — everyone underestimated by 20–40%
2. **Inertia is the enemy** — people keep paying even when unsatisfied, because switching feels risky
3. **Trust comes from transparency** — showing source URLs and explaining reasoning builds credibility
4. **Sharing is natural** — all 3 said they'd share results with someone (manager, team, Twitter)
5. **"Which one should I keep" > "here's how much you can save"** — action framing beats savings framing

## How These Insights Changed the Product

- Added "recommended action" field to each tool card (keep / downgrade / switch / optimize)
- Added source URLs to PRICING_DATA.md so users can verify
- Made shareable URL a first-class feature (not an afterthought)
- Changed results page headline from "You could save $X" to "Here's your optimized AI stack"
